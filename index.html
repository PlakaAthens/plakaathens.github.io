Explore the roots of Western civilization. These iconic monuments are just a breath away from the alleys of Plaka.

⭐ 4.9/5 Iconic Landmark

Acropolis of Athens

⚠️ Avoid the Mistake The ticket line in summer can exceed 2 hours in 40°C heat. Never buy tickets on-site during peak season.

Walk the sacred hill where gods, myths, and democracy collide—every step feels like stepping into eternity.

The Acropolis is not just a site—it’s the beating heart of ancient Greece. Crowned by the Parthenon, this UNESCO World Heritage monument offers breathtaking panoramic views over Athens. A guided experience unlocks the symbolism and power embedded in every stone.

⭐ 4.9/5 World Wonder

Parthenon

Stand beneath the most perfect temple ever built—and feel the power of ancient genius.

Dedicated to Athena, the Parthenon represents the peak of classical Greek architecture. Its mathematical precision still amazes engineers today. Up close, the scale and detail are staggering.

⭐ 4.8/5 Architectural Icon

Erechtheion

Meet the legendary Caryatids—the marble maidens holding up history itself.

This asymmetrical masterpiece marks sacred spots tied to Athena and Poseidon’s mythic rivalry. The Porch of the Caryatids is its crown jewel—graceful female statues serving as columns.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.7/5 Birthplace of Theater

Theatre of Dionysus

Sit where ancient Greeks watched the very first tragedies and comedies unfold.

This is the world’s oldest known theater. Legends like Sophocles premiered their works here. Imagine 17,000 spectators gathered under the open sky connecting to the origins of drama.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.9/5 Still in Use

Odeon of Herodes Atticus

A 2,000-year-old theater that still hosts concerts under the stars.

Built in Roman times, this beautifully restored amphitheater remains active today. Its acoustics are so perfect that even whispers carry. A must-see on the Acropolis slope.

🎟️ Festival tickets sell out months ahead

⭐ 4.7/5 Hidden Gem

Temple of Athena Nike

Small in size—massive in symbolism of victory and power.

Perched at the entrance of the Acropolis, this temple celebrates Athena as the goddess of victory. Despite its size, it commands incredible views over Athens.

Birthplace of Democracy
⭐ 4.8/5 Historical Heart

Ancient Agora of Athens

Walk through the gathering place where Socrates debated and democracy was born.

This was the city’s main social hub. You’ll explore ruins of temples, stoas, and public buildings. A guided tour transforms these ancient stones into stories.

⭐ 4.9/5 Best-Preserved Temple

Temple of Hephaestus

A nearly perfect ancient Greek temple—standing strong for over 2,400 years.

Dedicated to the god of craftsmanship, this Doric temple is one of the best-preserved in Greece. Surrounded by greenery, it feels almost untouched by time. Its detailed friezes depict heroic myths.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.6/5 Roman Legacy

Roman Agora

Discover Athens under Roman rule—where empires collided.

Built during Roman occupation, this marketplace replaced the Greek Agora. It reflects a shift in power and culture. The layout and ruins reveal a different side of Athens’ evolution.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.7/5 Ancient Technology

Tower of the Winds

An ancient weather station that predicted winds 2,000 years ago.

This octagonal marble tower functioned as a clock, compass, and weather vane. Each side represents a wind god. It’s one of the most unique structures in Athens.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.6/5 Imperial Athens

Hadrian's Library

A Roman emperor’s gift to Athens—knowledge, power, and prestige.

Built by Emperor Hadrian, this complex once housed scrolls, lecture halls, and gardens. Today, its ruins still hint at its grandeur. Included in the Athens combo tickets.

Buy Official Tickets
⭐ 4.8/5 Must-See Museum

Stoa of Attalos

Step inside a fully reconstructed ancient shopping arcade.

Rebuilt in the 1950s, this stoa gives a rare glimpse into ancient daily life. Inside, you’ll find artifacts from the Agora. It’s immersive and beautifully preserved.

Secret Island Village
⭐ 4.9/5 Most Romantic Spot

Anafiotika

A hidden island village… right beneath the Acropolis.

Built by island craftsmen in the 19th century, Anafiotika feels like the Cyclades. Whitewashed houses and narrow alleys create a magical contrast to busy Athens. Perfect for wandering.

📍 View on Map
⭐ 4.7/5 Byzantine Heritage

Church of Panagia Kapnikarea

A thousand-year-old church standing firm in the middle of modern chaos.

This 11th-century Byzantine church sits on busy Ermou Street. Its serene interior and frescoes offer a glimpse into medieval Athens. It’s a peaceful refuge from the city’s energy.

📍 View on Map
⭐ 4.6/5 Classical Ornament

Choragic Monument of Lysicrates

An elegant marble monument with a great story of victory and art.

Built to celebrate a theatrical victory, this is one of the first examples of the Corinthian order. Tucked in a scenic corner of Plaka, it’s worth a stop for its detail and history.

📍 View on Map
⭐ 4.5/5 Ottoman History

Fethiye Mosque

A rare Ottoman mosque hidden among ancient ruins.

Built during the Ottoman period, the mosque reflects a different chapter of Athens’ past within the Roman Agora. It’s a site of architectural and historical contrast.

⭐ 4.7/5 Cultural Depth

Byzantine Museum

Discover the "forgotten" empire behind Athens' classical fame.

Icons, mosaics, and artifacts tell the story of Byzantine Greece, adding depth to your understanding of the city’s history. It’s a masterclass in religious art and culture.

⭐ 4.9/5 Best Free View

Philopappos Hill

The most stunning (and free) view of the Acropolis in the city.

This hill offers panoramic views, especially at sunset. It’s less crowded than the Acropolis and a local favorite for walks. You’ll find historical monuments and secret paths.
